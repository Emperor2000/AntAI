Cell types

Our game area is a grid which consists of cells. Each cell acts as a container for the different entities that move across the map. Cells are static objects that define how the map/terrain behaves.

Empty cells ▮

Empty cells are the basic type of cell used for constructing the grid. Seeing a white square indicates a cell that does not contain any entities or unique properties, but rather, basic information that each cell contains.

Each cell contains a number of properties. The first properties, x and y are for navigation. A cell has an x and y position to represent it's location.

An empty cell is traversable by ants and contain a cost of {{globalvars.CELL_COSTS}}, but this cost is temporarily lowered if an ant recently traversed it. This rewards following a trait ants have in nature; they walk in a straight line and follow each other.

Food cells ▮

A food cell is traversable like an empty cell. However, it also allows gatherer ants to take {{globalvars.GATHERER_MAXFOODAMOUNT}} food out of it's food storage, which is a property contained in the food cell. Food cells gradually produce food over multiple turns.

Food cells change colour based on the amount of food that is present in the cell. A dark shade of green indicates a large yield is present, whereas a light shader indicates the food is running out.

Blockade cells ▮

Blockade cells are different from food cells and empty cells. Blockade cells cannot be traversed, and thus the cost for traversing it is irrelevant. The pathfinding algorithm (A* search) calculates a route around these blockades/obstacles.
